Once a professor creates and activates a game, students access it through the TecBooks student panel. The panel is organized around four main sections — **Inbox**, **My Games**, **Join Game**, and **Profile** — and connects directly to the financial dashboard where the core analysis work happens.

## Typical student workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in and open the student panel">
    Log in to TecBooks. You are directed to your institution's student panel at `/mxrep/{institution-slug}/student-panel`. If you have not yet joined a group, contact your professor to confirm your enrollment before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your inbox">
    Open **Inbox** from the sidebar. The inbox collects three types of messages from your professors:

    - **Game invitations** — an invitation to join a specific simulation game, with the game name and dates.
    - **Team assignments** — confirmation of which team you have been placed in for a game.
    - **Grade notifications** — a notice that results for a completed game are available to review.

    Each notification has a **Review** button that takes you directly to the relevant game.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Join a game">
    If you received a game invitation in your inbox, click **Review** to open it. Alternatively, navigate to **My Games** and click **Join Game**, then enter the game code your professor shared with you. Once joined, the game appears in your My Games list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access the financial dashboard and complete your analysis">
    Open the game from **My Games** to enter the game workspace. From there you can access the TecBooks financial dashboard, which contains the full set of analytical tools — financial statements, financial health indicators, productivity metrics, forecasts, and project evaluation — pre-loaded with the economic data your professor configured for this game.

    Work through the analysis with your team. Each team member can access the dashboard simultaneously.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View your results in My Games">
    After submitting or completing the game, return to **My Games** and open the game entry. Completed runs are stored there so you can review your outputs, compare with previous attempts, and check any grade or feedback your professor has posted.

    Use the **Active / All** toggle on the My Games page to switch between currently open games and your full game history.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Student panel sections

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Inbox">
    The inbox is your notification center. It shows all pending messages from professors, sorted by date. Notification types include game invitations, team assignments, and grade-available alerts. Notifications are not deleted after you review them, so you can return to the inbox to reference past communications at any time.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="My Games">
    My Games is the central hub for your simulation work. It lists every game you have joined, with each entry showing the game name and status. By default the view shows only **Active** games; switch to **All** to see inactive and completed entries. Click any game card to open the game workspace and access the financial dashboard.

    You can also join a new game directly from this page using the **Join Game** button, which opens a modal where you enter the game code.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Join Game">
    The Join Game flow accepts a game code provided by your professor. Enter the code to request access. Once the join is confirmed, the game appears in your My Games list with an Active status. If the code is not working, verify it with your professor, as codes are case-sensitive and tied to a specific group enrollment.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Profile">
    Profile Settings lets you view and update your personal information and institution details. Changes are saved immediately. Navigate here from the sidebar whenever you need to update your contact information or review your account details.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Info>
  The student panel is scoped to your institution. If you study at Tecnologico de Monterrey your panel URL will include your campus or program slug. Contact your professor or institution administrator if you cannot access the panel after logging in.
</Info>
